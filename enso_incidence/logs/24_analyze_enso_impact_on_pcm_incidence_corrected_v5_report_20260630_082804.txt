Purpose:
Analyze the association between El Niño/ENSO and annual national incidence of probable acute/subacute PCM using the available integrated record period.

Primary analytical period:
1998 - 2024 

ONI plot period:
1996 - 2025 

Correction relative to v1:
The v1 analysis used the final modeling dataset with 2,415 municipalities.
This corrected v5 analysis uses the full national municipality-year spatial layer and searches broadly for the most complete IBGE municipal population table before calculating annual national incidence.
This version fixes the national outcome-column detection by prioritizing pcm_records_under20_main, selects the widest available IBGE municipal population file by content coverage, and adds a publication-style Annual mean Oceanic Niño Index plot.

Methodological note:
The integrated SIM + SIH outcome uses the common period available for both systems.
ENSO is a year-level exposure; therefore, the primary analysis is national annual rather than municipality-year independent modeling.
Counts are modeled with a population offset and a linear year term.
The analysis is exploratory and should not be interpreted as causal proof.

Interpretation text:
ENSO and national probable acute/subacute PCM incidence analysis
===============================================================

This analysis evaluated the association between El Niño/ENSO and annual national incidence of probable acute/subacute paracoccidioidomycosis in Brazil using the common integrated SIM + SIH period 1998-2024.

Important methodological note
-----------------------------
ENSO is a year-level climatic exposure. Therefore, the primary analysis was performed at the national annual level rather than by treating municipality-year rows as independent ENSO observations. The corrected v5 analysis uses the full national municipality-year grid and searches broadly for a population file with the greatest municipality-year coverage, rather than relying only on the embedded or restricted denominator file. Counts were modeled with a population offset, and the main models included a linear calendar-year term to reduce confounding by long-term temporal trends. Because the annual time series is short, results should be interpreted as exploratory evidence of association, not as causal proof.

Annual national dataset summary
-------------------------------
Years analyzed: 27 (1998-2024)
Mean municipalities per year: 5570.0
Total events: 1,282
Median annual national incidence: 0.0241 per 100,000
Mean annual national incidence: 0.0243 per 100,000

Main quasi-Poisson estimates
----------------------------
Annual mean ONI, same year: IRR 1.042 (95% CI 0.864-1.256), p = 0.6722
Annual mean ONI, 1-year lag: IRR 1.170 (95% CI 1.003-1.365), p = 0.0569
El Niño phase, same year versus Neutral: IRR 1.162 (95% CI 0.832-1.621), p = 0.3875
El Niño phase, 1-year lag versus Neutral: IRR 1.277 (95% CI 0.972-1.677), p = 0.0918

Interpretation guidance
-----------------------
If IRR values are above 1, they suggest higher annual incidence during or after warmer ENSO conditions; if below 1, they suggest lower annual incidence. Confidence intervals crossing 1 indicate substantial statistical uncertainty. Lagged ONI models are biologically plausible because PCM clinical records may occur after environmental exposure, infection, disease progression, diagnosis, and hospitalization or death registration.
